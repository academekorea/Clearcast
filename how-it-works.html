How it works

From URL to full bias report
in under two minutes

Here's exactly what happens when you submit a podcast or YouTube URL to Podlens — and why it works the way it does.

01

Paste a URL

Works with any YouTube video or direct podcast audio link. We extract the video ID and check whether the video is public and long enough to be meaningful (typically 5+ minutes).

02

We transcribe the audio

We attempt to use YouTube's built-in captions first (fastest path). If those aren't available, we fall back to Apple Podcasts and Podcast Index to find a clean audio file, then transcribe it using AI transcription. The full audio is never stored — only the transcript text.

03

Our AI reads the transcript

We send the transcript to our analysis engine with a detailed media literacy prompt. The model is instructed to identify loaded language, positional framing, unchallenged claims, and missing perspectives — and to ground every assessment in specific, quotable moments.

04

Results delivered in two phases

Phase 1 (seconds): headline metrics appear immediately — the bias bar, factuality rating, omission risk, and host trust score. Phase 2 (15–30 seconds): deep analysis fills in — topic breakdown, key quotes, missing voices, sponsor conflicts, and flags with source citations.

The bias scale

How to read the bias bar

Every analysis produces a three-part bar — left, center, right — based on framing signals detected in the actual words.

Mostly balanced
0–19% lean in either direction. The episode covers topics without strong positional framing.
Lightly one-sided
20–39% lean. A clear perspective but still largely informational.
Moderately biased
40–59% lean. Strong framing choices that consistently favor one worldview.
Heavily one-sided
60–79% lean. Opposing perspectives are largely absent or dismissed.
Extremely one-sided
80–100% lean. The content is essentially advocacy, not reporting.
Left lean signals
Emphasis on systemic inequality, progressive policy framing, unchallenged climate urgency claims, corporate skepticism, identity-focused language.
Right lean signals
Free-market framing, skepticism of government intervention, traditional values language, immigration restriction framing, critique of mainstream media.
Balanced signals
Multiple perspectives given roughly equal airtime, factual claims cited, clear distinction between opinion and reporting, no consistent positional framing.

Frequently asked questions

Is this tool politically biased itself?
We use the same methodology regardless of political direction. The AI is instructed to identify framing signals — not to favor any ideology. We've tested it across content from across the political spectrum.
Can it analyze any podcast?
It works best with YouTube videos and podcast episodes with publicly available audio. Private videos, live streams, and content shorter than 5 minutes aren't currently supported.
Is my data stored?
Transcripts are temporarily stored for caching (7 days) so you don't have to re-transcribe the same episode. Your analysis history lives in your browser and is never sent to our servers unless you're logged in.
How accurate is it?
Podlens is a starting point for critical listening — not a verdict. The AI can miss irony, satire, and specialized context. We recommend reading the source citations and making your own judgement.
Does Podlens use ads or sell data?
No. We're funded entirely by subscriber revenue. We have no advertising relationships and we don't sell or share user data.
