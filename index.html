The intelligence layer

6 dimensions on every episode

All 6 scores on every tier. Depth of transcript evidence varies — not access to the scores.
01
Political lean
Left / center / right framing, language patterns, source selection bias. Scored −100 to +100 with transcript evidence.
02
Factual density
Sourced claims ÷ total claims. How often does the host cite evidence vs state opinion as fact?
03
Source diversity
Distinct perspectives cited, expert balance. Are opposing views ever heard, or only one side of the story?
04
Framing patterns
Loaded language frequency, emotional vs neutral ratio, repetition of unqualified assertions.
05
Host credibility
How rigorously the host challenges claims. Separate guest score shows how hard the guest was pushed.
06
Omission risk
What’s absent vs comparable coverage? The story not told is often more important than the one that is.

Common questions

Is my listening data private?
Transcripts are cached for 7 days so you don’t re-transcribe the same episode. Your analysis history lives in your browser unless you’re signed in. No advertising relationships. No data sales. Ever.
How accurate is it?
Podlens is a starting point for critical listening — not a verdict. The AI can miss irony, satire, and specialized context. We benchmark against human-labeled episodes and publish our methodology openly.
Does it work with YouTube videos that aren’t podcasts?
Yes — any YouTube video, news segment, lecture, or interview is analyzed identically to a podcast. The channel name becomes the “show” and all 6 dimensions apply the same way.
Does Podlens use ads or sell data?
No. Funded entirely by subscriber revenue. No advertising relationships, no data sales, no conflicts of interest.
